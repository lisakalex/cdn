  3. Hybrid Integration

    • Mild-hybrid systems (like EQ Boost) pair more easily with turbos than superchargers.

Closest Modern Equivalent

  • Mercedes now uses something called an electric auxiliary compressor in some AMG models (e.g., AMG 53 series).

Great question, Alex. The 9G-Tronic is Mercedes-Benz’s 9-speed automatic gearbox. It replaced the old 7G-Tronic and is now used across most of their lineup, including the C200. Here’s how it works in plain English:

1. Core design

  • It’s a hydraulic torque-converter automatic (not dual-clutch, not CVT).

  • Inside are four planetary gear sets and six shift elements (clutches and brakes). By engaging different combinations, it creates 9 forward ratios plus reverse.

  • Ratios are very close in the middle gears, but very tall in 9th — this keeps the engine quiet and efficient on the motorway.

2. Torque Converter

  • When you pull away from a stop, the torque converter connects engine to gearbox with some slip.

  • At higher speeds, it locks up with a clutch to eliminate slippage and improve efficiency.

  • This is why it feels very smooth, but also why it doesn’t “snap” off the line like your C230 manual.

3. Electronics & Control

  • Controlled by a unit called TCU (Transmission Control Unit).

  • It constantly checks throttle, speed, load, incline, and driver mode (Comfort, Sport, Sport+) to decide when to shift.

  • In Comfort, it upshifts as early as possible for fuel economy. In Sport/Sport+, it holds gears longer, shifts quicker, and is more aggressive with kickdown.

4. Paddles & Manual Mode

  • You have two paddles:

    • Left (-) = shift down

    • Right (+) = shift up

  • In “manual” mode, it obeys your paddle commands (unless it risks stalling or over-revving).

  • The gearbox still uses the torque converter, so shifts aren’t as sharp as a dual-clutch, but they’re smoother and more durable.

5. Integration with Mild-Hybrid (EQ Boost)

  • In cars like the C200 MHEV, a belt-driven starter-generator (BSG) adds up to 14–20 hp during pull-off and can “fill in” torque during shifts.

  • This helps mask turbo lag and makes gear changes feel seamless.

6. Advantages

  • Smooth and quiet at all times.

  • Excellent fuel economy (the tall 9th gear keeps revs low on the motorway).

  • Can handle high torque (same gearbox used in big diesels and AMG models).

7. Downsides

  • Sometimes feels “lazy” in stop-start driving — because it prioritises comfort and smoothness over raw response.

  • More complex than a manual → higher repair costs if anything goes wrong.
